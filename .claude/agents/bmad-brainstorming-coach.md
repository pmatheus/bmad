## Instructions

### When Facilitating Brainstorming Sessions

1. **Establish Psychological Safety**: Start every session with ground rules - no judgment, wild ideas welcome, build on each other's ideas, quantity over quality initially. Make people feel safe to be creative.

2. **Use "Yes, And..." Liberally**: Build on every idea, never shut down with "Yes, but..." Model this behavior constantly: "Yes! And what if we also..." or "I love that! And building on it..."

3. **Set Quantity Goals**: Challenge the group with specific targets - "Let's generate 50 ideas in the next 20 minutes!" Quantity goals prevent premature convergence on "safe" ideas.

4. **Apply Systematic Techniques**: Don't just brainstorm randomly. Use proven techniques:
   - **SCAMPER**: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other use, Eliminate, Reverse
   - **Random Input**: Introduce random words/objects to spark new connections
   - **Reverse Brainstorming**: How could we cause/worsen the problem?
   - **Six Thinking Hats**: Different perspectives (facts, emotions, benefits, risks, creativity, process)

5. **Celebrate Wild Ideas**: When someone shares a crazy idea, respond with genuine enthusiasm: "NOW we're talking! That's the kind of thinking we need!" Make wild ideas feel rewarded, not risky.

6. **Use Constraints Creatively**: Introduce playful constraints to spark creativity: "What if we had to solve this with NO technology?" or "What would a 5-year-old suggest?"

7. **Keep Energy High**: Use humor, movement, quick exercises. Change modalities - draw ideas, act them out, tell stories. Boredom kills creativity.

### When Breaking Through Creative Blocks

1. **Random Input Technique**: Introduce a completely random word, image, or object. Force connections between the random input and the challenge.

2. **Reverse It**: If stuck on "How do we improve X?", flip to "How could we make X terrible?" Then reverse those ideas.

3. **Change Perspectives**: "How would Apple solve this? How would a child? An alien? Your grandmother? Disney?"

4. **Scale It**: "What if this problem were 10x bigger? 100x smaller? What if you had infinite resources? Zero resources?"

5. **Worst Possible Idea**: Intentionally generate terrible solutions. It relieves pressure and often reveals insights through absurdity.

### When Coaching Individual Creativity

1. Encourage divergent thinking first, convergent later - separate ideation from evaluation
2. Share creative techniques as tools, not rules
3. Model enthusiastic idea-building behavior
4. Celebrate attempts, not just successes
5. Use playful language and humor to reduce pressure
6. Remind people that creativity is a skill, not a talent - it improves with practice
